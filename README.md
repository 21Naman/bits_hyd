## PROBLEM — (10 pts)

India's MSMEs are the backbone of our economy. Nearly 7 crore MSMEs contribute nearly 30% of our GDP, employ over 110 million people, and drive nearly half our exports. And yet — they are chronically starved of working capital.

*(Pause. Let that land.)*

- **80%+** of MSMEs face delayed payments
- **₹25 trillion** — the MSME credit gap, per the IFC
- **₹10.7 lakh crore** locked in receivables every single year

Now here's the irony. Many of these same MSMEs — the factory owner, the warehouse operator, the industrial unit runner — they *own* significant commercial and industrial real estate. Their balance sheets are loaded with fixed assets. But that wealth is frozen. It can't pay salaries. It can't buy raw materials. It can't fund growth.

Their only option today? Sell the whole property — often at a distressed price — or don't sell at all and keep struggling. There is no middle path.

**That is the problem we are solving.**

## SOLUTION — (10 pts)

We are building **Fractify** — a platform that allows MSMEs to list a fraction of their commercial or industrial real estate on a regulated marketplace, and have retail investors co-own it through a Special Purpose Vehicle.

> Think of it this way — SEBI regulates how companies raise capital from the public. Fractify does the same for real estate. The property is the company. Our platform is SEBI. The SPV is the listed entity.

**Here is exactly how it works:**

`MSME lists property` → `SPV is created` → `Full asset transferred to SPV` → `MSME retains X% units` → `Remaining % sold to retail investors` → `MSME pays rent on investor-owned %, distributed to unit holders`

For example — a factory owner with a ₹10 crore industrial unit lists 30% on Fractify. That stake is tokenised into units inside an SPV. Retail investors buy these units. The factory owner continues to operate the factory, paying market rent on the 30% he's sold. That rent is distributed to investors proportionally. The MSME gets immediate liquidity — without losing operational control or giving up his business.

### The trust layer — blockchain infrastructure

Under the hood, every unit of ownership is a tokenised record on a permissioned blockchain — making ownership immutable, rent distribution automatic via smart contracts, and every transaction auditable in real time. No spreadsheets. No intermediaries. No disputes.

Smart contracts handle rent collection and payout on a fixed cycle. Every ownership transfer, every payout, every valuation update is recorded permanently — visible to the investor, the MSME, and our compliance team. This isn't a marketing feature. It is the infrastructure that makes trust possible at scale.

### Listing criteria an asset must meet:

- Commercial or industrial use only — no residential
- Clear, unencumbered title deed
- Minimum asset value of ₹1 crore
- Revenue-generating or already tenanted property
- MSME must retain a minimum 50% ownership stake
- No pending litigation or regulatory disputes

## MARKET UNDERSTANDING — (10 pts)

SEBI introduced its SM REIT framework in March 2024, recognising that the fractional ownership market covers over 300 million sq ft of potential commercial real estate. REITs and InvITs together mobilised ₹1.3 trillion between FY2019–20 and March 2024.

But here's what every existing player — Property Share, hBits, Strata — is doing: they target *investors* looking for premium Grade-A office space. They source the asset, find the capital, and manage returns.

Nobody is going to the **supply side** — the MSME that already owns the asset and desperately needs capital. That is our entry point. That is our moat.

## USP — (10 pts)

Every competitor starts with a property and finds investors. We start with an MSME owner and create a two-sided market.

**For the MSME seller:** partial liquidity without losing the business. No distressed sale. No collateral loan. No more choosing between survival and ownership.

**For the retail investor:** entry into commercial real estate with low ticket sizes, rental income from day one, and capital appreciation — an asset class previously accessible only to HNIs and institutions.

**For trust:** unlike unregulated FOPs of the past, we operate within the SEBI SM REIT framework — giving every investor mandatory disclosures, listed liquidity, and grievance redressal. And with blockchain at the infrastructure layer, ownership and payouts are transparent, tamper-proof, and verifiable by anyone, at any time.

Three things working together — SEBI compliance, SPV structure, and blockchain infrastructure — that no competitor has combined for the MSME segment.

## REVENUE STREAMS — (10 pts)

**1. Platform listing fee (primary)**
Annual SaaS-style fee from MSMEs to list and manage their asset on Fractify. Recurring, predictable revenue — the MSME pays to access liquidity.

**2. AUM management fee**
A small percentage of assets under management per scheme — aligned with the SEBI SM REIT model used by Property Share and hBits. Scales directly with platform growth.

**3. SPV structuring & compliance fee**
One-time fee for SPV formation, independent valuation, and regulatory filing. Analogous to IPO underwriting — charged per listing.

*Note on transaction fees: We are benchmarking against the SEBI model, where the regulator charges registration and annual fees rather than per-trade commissions. Keeping investor-side costs low builds trust and drives adoption — which grows our AUM fee base over time.*
